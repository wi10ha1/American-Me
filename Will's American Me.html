Family Story

    In 1862, Thomas Halloran and some of his brothers were arrested for the murder of Gustave Thiebault. Thomas’s brothers were permitted to leave custody, but Thomas had to stay and take the murder charge on his own. Whether innocent or guilty, Thomas knew that the odds were better for eviction than acquittal, and if convicted it would be death by hanging.
   The Daily Express reported that Thomas received the “not guilty” verdict and the crowd cheered; however, no one knew if the cheers were for the belief of Tom’s innocence or by distaste for the victim. Thomas never discussed the case with any of his family and no one was ever charged with the crime, so the question of innocence with Thomas Halloran is still unknown.
